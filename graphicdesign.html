Heritage Stamp

I did a stamp about my great-grandmother, who was in the first group of temple workers in the Buenos Aires Temple in 1986. Her name was Ligia, but we called her Lele, so I used her nickname for the stamp.


In my design, I aligned the visual elements to follow the Z pattern. The name is in the upper-left corner, and then comes a little of white space and then the view comes to the lower left corner, where the image and the flowers are, and finally to the lower right corner, where the country and price stamp is. I also followed the color contrast advice; I used blue and red. The flowers comply with the rule of odds; there are three big objects overlapping with the frame and the pic. Finally, I played with the size of the elements to emphasize information, like the name, and brought attention to details, like the flowers, in the picture.

Personal Brand Sticker

I used a monochromatic color scheme inspired by the colors in my portfolio website. I used a Y shape with rounded corners, and I repeated it several times in different colors of the monochromatic scale I was using, making one bigger than the next to create a layered effect. I added a paper texture clipping mask. As far as design principles, there is the alignment of the shapes to create the effect, the contrast of the colors that go from darkest to lightest, and the repetition of the Y shape.
