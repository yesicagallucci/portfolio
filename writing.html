Top 10 Movies Where the Real Victory Is a Change of Heart

By Yesica Gallucci - July 18, 2026

I love stories, I love reading them, watching TV series, social media, movies, and even gossip. I learn something from each story that helps me grow. I learn about behaviors, society, struggles, empathy, and a lot more.
Ryan Niemiec opened his article “Character strengths cinematherapy: Using movies to inspire change, meaning, and cinematic elevation” with this: “Good movies have the potential to capture a trifecta of meaning. Meaningful movie experience can be enjoyable, engaging, and empowering. In other words, we experience pleasure, focused attention, and concentration, and are inspired toward the good or reach new insights into ourselves, others, or the human condition.” (Niemiec, 2020, para. 1)

I want to share my top 10 list of movies that helped me learn resilience and how to embrace change while facing adversity or a core-forming experience.

1. Larry Crowne

After facing economic troubles, Larry had to change his life from what he had planned; he adapted to a new reality, letting go of old ideas of success, and, in doing so, his life improved.

2. Waffle Street

After being fired from his dream job, James starts a career searching journey and a self-discovery process that changes his goals and mindset.

3. Three Idiots

Rancho was the catalyst for his friend's journeys; he helped them grow and make difficult decisions. He taught them about freedom, gratitude, bravery, and kindness.

4. Encanto

Mirabel struggles to find her worth in a family built around magical gifts, believing she has nothing to offer. Through her journey, she discovers that connection and courage matter more than any gift and helps her family redefine what makes someone valuable.

5. Groundhog Day

Phil starts out self-centered and dismissive of everyone around him, trapped in a day, he tries everything until he accepts his fate and starts living the day blessing others.

6. Confessions of a Shopaholic

Rebecca hides her financial chaos behind a glamorous image, believing appearances are what matter most. As her secrets unravel, she learns that honesty and real relationships are worth more than any purchase.

7. Little Giants

Becky is a tween who is trying to find her place in the world, and in the process, she will need to make decisions that will mark what kind of person she is becoming.

8. Front of the Class

Brad shows courage and determination to fulfill his dream of teaching despite his Tourette's, and in the process, he teaches others empathy and acceptance.

9. About a Boy

Will lives a shallow, disconnected life, avoiding responsibility and real relationships. Through his bond with Marcus, he discovers that genuine connection is worth more than the freedom he thought he wanted.

10. A Walk to Remember

Landon starts out chasing reputation and rebellion to fit in with his peers. Through his relationship with Jamie, he learns that character and love matter more than image, and it changes the direction of his life.

It has been a long time since I watched some of these movies, but I know that if you watch them, you’ll connect with the characters' experiences, broaden your capacity to feel empathy, and you’ll learn valuable lessons about facing changes and navigating adversities.

Source

Niemiec, R. M. (2020). Character strengths cinematherapy: Using movies to inspire change, meaning, and cinematic elevation. Journal of Clinical Psychology, 1–16. https://doi.org/10.1002/jclp.22997
